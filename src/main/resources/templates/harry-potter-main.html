Harry Potter: Magic World


Creating a story


If we look at the whole story from above, in general, we immediately see several key features. Firstly, each book is one year in the life of the Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. All the books (except the last one) follow a pattern: summer at the Dursleys, train to Hogwarts, lessons and adventures, Christmas, exams, battle, departure for the holidays. The first two chapters are a summary of the "story so far" for new readers - up to the sixth book. There Rowling decided that people brave enough to start the series of books from the middle can figure it out themselves. Secondly, all the titles are written according to the formula "X and Y of Z" - Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban, for example. And this technique has become the hallmark of the Joan Rowling franchise and a marker of plagiarism - the same books by Dmitry Yemets immediately "screamed" that they were a copy of Harry Potter, because they were "Tanya Grotter and ...".


Harry Potter is a pretty textbook story in terms of characters and their archetypes.



We have the Protagonist (Harry), who is going through his Hero's Journey - a terrible event has torn him out of "paradise" and now he goes through various trials on his way to get back there. His best friends - Ron and Hermione, as well as secondary supporting characters (Hogwarts students, the Weasleys, the Order of the Phoenix) help the protagonist achieve his goal. The protagonist also has several enemies - Draco Malfoy, Severus Snape and Lord Voldemort (in gradation from less dangerous to more). Voldemort is the Big Bad, a great evil that puts in danger not only Harry and his friends, but the whole world, and, of course, only Harry can defeat him. But the Big Evil is opposed by the Big Good in the person of Professor Dumbledore, one of the greatest wizards of this world.


That is, if you need to study a standard and understandable to most epic plot, you can take "Harry Potter" as a basis without a doubt.



This is due to the fact that Rowling is a philologist by education, and she formed her style on classical works and fairy tales, which often have a similar, albeit less global, structure. (Remember "The Lord of the Rings").


Of course, if you look at the composition in detail, you can see many more elements that are repeated from book to book, for example, in the first five books, Harry always meets (or at least learns about the new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher) before the start of classes. And he already knew the sixth one.



Or that in each book there is a red herring - a hint that leads us to a false solution, as, for example, in the second book, Harry kept thinking that it was Malfoy, the heir of Slytherin, and he opened the Chamber of Secrets. And in the first one, he suspected Snow. Write in the comments what common moments in each book you saw.


Rowling initially planned exactly this length and complexity of the story - that's why there are a lot of "Chekhov's guns" in it - seemingly insignificant moments at first, which "shoot" only later and play a very important role in the plot. For example, Tom Riddle's Diary, the rat Scabbers, Sirius Black or Bellatrix Lestrange. Joan Rowling carefully protected her fairy tale from spoilers and revealed the main secret only to Alan Rickman, who played Snape - he himself asked about it, in order to better understand the character's motivation.


And one of the confirmations is the most obvious hint to the denouement of the 7th book - in the first book, at the very beginning of the story, Ollivander says: "The wand chooses the wizard, Harry" Just think about it now!



It is also obvious to the naked eye that from the fourth book, the moment when the first death occurs, the series becomes more mature and darker. This is reflected in both the colors of the film and the written soundtrack. Readers become older, events in the universe become more frightening, the Second Wizarding War begins.


But, as in any good fairy tale, good will definitely triumph over evil. And love will light the world even in the darkest times.


The magical world and its inhabitants Any work of art creates its own universe, which differs from the real one, be it the Marvel universe or a light rom-com with Reese Witherspoon. However, in the case of Marvel, it is easier for us to understand that this world is unreal. Therefore, let's start by defining what is so special about the multifaceted Harry Potter universe and by what laws it exists. Firstly, we do not see so many magical places where wizards are concentrated. This is Hogwarts, the Ministry of Magic, Diagon Alley, Hogsmeade, St. Mungo's, Gringotts and that's it. Where, for example, the Burrow or Shell Cottage (where Bill and Fleur live) are, we don't know, but it seems that many wizards live among Muggles, judging by Godric's Hollow or, for example, the Ryld, Evans and Snape families.
